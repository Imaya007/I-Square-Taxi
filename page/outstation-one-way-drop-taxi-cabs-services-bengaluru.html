Bangalore, also known as Bengaluru, is the capital city of the Indian state of Karnataka and is known for its rich history and culture. The city is a popular tourist destination and is home to several popular destinations that are worth visiting.

One of the most popular destinations in Bangalore is Lalbagh Botanical Garden, which is a 240-acre park that is home to a wide variety of plant species, including a collection of rare and exotic plants. The park also features a beautiful glass house, a lake, and a variety of statues and monuments.

Another popular destination in Bangalore is the ISKCON Temple, which is one of the largest ISKCON temples in the world. The temple is a beautiful and peaceful place and is a popular spot for both tourists and locals.

The Cubbon Park is also a popular destination in Bangalore, it is a 300-acre park that is home to a wide variety of plant species, and it also features a children's playground, a lake, and several statues and monuments.

The Bangalore Palace is a beautiful palace that was built in 1887, it is a popular tourist destination and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.

The Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace is also a popular destination in Bangalore, it is a beautiful palace that was built in the 18th century by the ruler of Mysore, Tipu Sultan. The palace is a popular spot for both tourists and locals and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.

Places to visit in Bengaluru

There is a number of popular places in Bengaluru which attracts people and tourist like:

 

Lal Bagh Botanical garden in Bengaluru

Lalbagh or Lalbagh Botanical Gardens, meaning The Red Garden in English, is a well-known botanical garden in southern Bengaluru, India. It has a famous glass house dating from 1889 which hosts two annual flower shows.

 

Bannerghatta National Park

Bannerghatta National Park, near Bengaluru, Karnataka, was founded in 1970 and declared as a national park in 1974. In 2002 a portion of the park, became a biological reserve, the Bannerghatta Biological Park

 

Cubbon Park

Cubbon Park Officially called Sri Chamarajendra Park is a landmark 'lung' area of the Bengaluru city, located within the heart of the city in the Central Administrative Area.

 

Bengaluru Palace

Bengaluru Palace, a palace located in Bengaluru, Karnataka, India, in an area owned originally by Rev. J. Garrett, the first principal of the Central High School in Bengaluru, now famous as Central College. The commencement of the construction of the palace is attributed to him.

 

ISKCON Temple Bengaluru

Sri Radha Krishna Temple has deities of Radha and Krishna located at Rajajinagar, in North Bengaluru, Karnataka, India. It is one of the largest ISKCON temples in the world. The temple is a huge cultural complex that was inaugurated in 1997 by Shankar Dayal Sharma.

 

Vidhana Soudha

The Vidhana Soudha located in Bengaluru is the seat of the state legislature of Karnataka. It is constructed in a style sometimes described as Mysore Neo-Dravidian and incorporates elements of Indo-Saracenic and Dravidian styles. The construction was completed in 1956.
